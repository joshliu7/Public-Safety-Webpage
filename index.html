Mass Ave Public Safety

Data visualizations to help improve public safety along Mass Ave in the stretch between Huntington Ave and Boston Medical Center.





By: Milan Ghosh, Joshua Liu, Ali Moeinzad

Motivation

The goal of this project is to help improve public safety along Massachusetts Avenue from Huntington Ave to Boston Medical Center through data analysis and visualizations. This region is well known for being afflicted by the opioid crisis, and we expect this to have a significant effect on the crime and public safety data. This project will examine the relationships between distinct types of crime and public safety issues, and the specific areas in which they occur in order to better understand why issues happen where they do. This will hopefully assist the Mass Ave Coalition in their efforts to make the region a safer environment for all residents and visitors.




Data

The city of Boston collects and publicizes datasets on Analyze Boston (data.boston.gov), self-described as “the City of Boston's open data hub to find facts, figures, and maps related to our lives within the city”. It provides access to at least 200 datasets, collected by various systems and departments in the city, and makes it easy to interact with and download the data.


We focused on two datasets, “Crime Incident Reports” and “311 Service Requests”, as these are both highly relevant to public safety. Criminal activity is a fundamental component of public safety, and the 311 database contains records of public nuisance reports, many of which can indirectly contribute to the sense of safety people will feel. Importantly, this will contain the data on needle pickup locations, which is extremely relevant to the state of public safety in this region. The data used in this report was for the period from January 1, 2021 to October 17, 2021.

Design Process

Sketches and design process

Since some of our tasks deal with the question of location we decided that creating a map to answer all those questions would be the best option. On our map we wanted to answer the questions of where the most crime occurs and where you can find the most needle trash? We decided the simplest and most effective way to show these would be by creating a point for each recorded instance of a crime occuring. Since we are plotting multiple different crimes we need some way to differentiate them and we decided that the best way to do this would be by color coding the points.

One of our most important domain tasks is which crime occurs the most? To answer this question we wanted to create a bar graph as they are an extremely effective visualization. We wanted each bar to be a type of crime and then the length of the bar to be the number of times that crime occurs. To make it even easier to read we planned on ordering the bar graph from highest to lowest and then we also wanted to color code the different bars and crimes to match the color scheme of our map.

Data Analysis

Interesting Results

The opioid epidemic has had an outsized effect on Massachusetts Avenue, where one can find many homeless people along well-known portions of the street. This population is reflected in the crime incident data from BPD. The graph below depicts the top 10 crime incidents in this region, using the “Incident Description” column of the crime dataset. The top three crime incidents of “Investigate Person”, “Sick Assist”, and “Sick/Injured/Medical – Person” follow the theme of people who stand out because they appear either dangerous, in need of medical attention, or both. This will come as no surprise to anyone who frequents this area of the street. However, the frequency of the first two incidents in comparison with the other eight does indicate the severity of the issue. Also, the fact that “Investigate Person” is ranked first shows the suspicion and fear that the reporters of these incidents feel, which will have a very strong effect on the sense of safety that people will feel in this area. The following two incidents in the chart, “Assault – Simple” and “Assault – Aggravated” represent the clear and obvious dangers of walking down this street. These are actual violent crimes that justify the sense of danger people experience down the street. This data provides validation for people who feel unsafe in the area, there is actual danger and risk.


On the map it can be seen that crimes are generally evenly spread out throughout the area, but there is clearly a higher concentration of crimes occurring in the bottom half of Mass Ave that is seen in the visualization. This logically makes sense because the top half is in the vicinity of Northeastern University’s campus, where there is a high concentration of students. Another takeaway is that assaults, both aggravated and simple, are especially common in the area around the Boston Medical Center, which shows that this area needs priority when addressing the crime issue. However, the main takeaway from this map is that there is a lot of crime in general and there is a very even spread of crimes and types of crimes throughout the area, which proves that action needs to be taken to address the crime issue along all of Mass Ave. There should be slightly more of a focus on the bottom half of Mass Ave., but the entire area needs a lot of attention in general.

Conclusion

We started off by identifying the most important domain task questions. In order to help answer these questions we created a visualization consisting of a map of crime incidents, a bar graph showing the number of occurrences for each type of crime, and a map showing where needle trash has been found. The visualization supports interactivity that allows for functions such as filtering the data, navigating around the map, and showing more information through popups. The visualization can ultimately be utilized to help identify trends and patterns in public safety issues around Mass Ave. If any improvements were to be made to this project the first thing to do would be to a slider for the time of day. This would allow for further in depth analysis of when and where crimes occur. After that the next thing to add would be another map of all the 311 data other than needle trash. Needle trash is an important public safety issue which is why we created a map for it, but there are plenty of other public safety issues in the 311 data set that we didn't include.
